About Bananue:

Banaue is a town in Ifugao, Cordillera Administrative Region, best known for the Banaue Rice Terraces, often called the “Eighth Wonder of the World.” These terraces were carved into the mountains over 2,000 years ago by the Ifugao people using traditional farming methods,and they are still used today. The town preserves rich indigenous culture, including Ifugao rituals, crafts, and traditions. Tourism in Banaue focuses on the terraces, local culture, and trekking to nearby villages. It’s also a hub for learning about sustainable farming and heritage conservation.

Culture:

Banaue is the ancestral home of the Ifugao people, whose way of life is closely connected to the world-famous Banaue Rice Terraces. Their culture revolves around rice cultivation, and they perform rituals and ceremonies to honor the land, ancestors, and spirits, especially during planting and harvesting seasons. Traditional arts like weaving, wood carving, and woodcraft are still practiced, showcasing their rich craftsmanship. Storytelling, folklore, and oral traditions preserve their history and beliefs across generations. Festivals and community gatherings celebrate both spiritual life and social unity, emphasizing respect for elders, cooperation, and harmony with nature. Overall, Banaue’s culture is a blend of ancestral heritage, sustainable living, and close-knit community values that continue to thrive today.Tourism has also helped share their culture with the world, bringing visitors closer to Ifugao traditions.

Foods:

1.Pinikpikan- A traditional chicken dish where the chicken is lightly beaten before cooking, giving it a unique flavor.
2.Etag- Cured or smoked pork, often used in stews or grilled dishes, with a distinct Ifugao taste.
3.Binungor- A local delicacy made from sticky rice mixed with coconut milk and sometimes wrapped in banana leaves.
